FIRE INJURY

Burns:

1. Stop the burning process: Move the victim away from the source of the fire.
2. Cool the burn: Run cool tap water over the burn for 10-15 minutes or apply a cool compress. Never use ice or ice water.
3. Remove clothing and jewelry: Gently remove any clothing or jewelry near the burned area, as they can stick to the burn.
4. Apply a topical ointment: Apply a topical antibiotic ointment, such as Bacitracin or Neosporin, to the burned area.
5. Cover the burn: Cover the burn with a non-stick, sterile bandage or dressing.

Inhalation Injuries:

1. Move to fresh air: Move the victim to fresh air immediately.
2. Monitor breathing: Monitor the victim's breathing and watch for signs of distress.
3. Provide oxygen: If available, provide oxygen according to medical instructions.

General First Aid:

1. Call for help: Call 108 or your local emergency number immediately.
2. Check for injuries: Check the victim for other injuries, such as smoke inhalation, broken bones, or head trauma.
3. Provide comfort: Keep the victim calm and comfortable until medical help arrives.

Remember:

- Never use butter, oil, or other greasy substances on burns, as they can cause further damage.
- Never break blisters or puncture wounds.
- Never give the victim anything to eat or drink unless they are conscious and alert.

It's always best to err on the side of caution and seek medical attention immediately if you're unsure of the severity of the injury.
